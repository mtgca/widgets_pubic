Transformer Training: Teacher Forcing & the Decoder Causal Mask

How does an encoder–decoder Transformer learn to translate “The cat sat on the mat” into “Le chat s'est assis sur le tapis”? Follow one training pair end to end: how the target sequence is shifted into a decoder input and a loss target, why variable-length batches need padding masks, and how a single upper-triangular causal mask turns one 8-token sequence into 8 training examples computed in one parallel forward pass.

1 · Data preparation

One target sentence, two tensors — the shift trick

The decoder is trained to predict the next French token given the previous ones. So wrap the target sentence with <SOS> and <EOS>, then build two aligned tensors from it: the decoder input (everything except the last token — “shifted right”) and the target for the loss (everything except the first token — “shifted left”). Both have length 8, and position t of the input must predict position t of the target.

Why padding? Batches mix sentence lengths

Real batches contain sentences of different lengths, but a tensor must be rectangular. Shorter sequences are padded with <PAD> to the longest length in the batch, and a padding mask marks which positions are real (1) and which are padding (0) so attention never looks at <PAD> — and the loss skips padded targets (e.g. ignore_index in PyTorch).

2 · Model processing

Teacher forcing: the decoder reads the ground truth, not its own guesses

The encoder consumes the full, unshifted English sentence with bidirectional self-attention — no shifting, no causal mask. The decoder receives the shifted French input and, through cross-attention, looks at the encoder output at every step. During training the decoder's input is always the ground-truth prefix — even when the model just made a mistake at the previous position. That is teacher forcing.

4 · Output & loss

All eight predictions hit one cross-entropy loss — in parallel

Each row of the decoder output is a probability distribution over the French vocabulary. Cross-entropy charges each position the negative log-probability it assigned to the correct token, then averages over positions (skipping any <PAD> targets):

L= −1T ∑t=1T log⁡ pθ ( yt ∣ y<t ,x )

5 · Scope & check yourself

What is decoder-only, and what is not

Padding mask ≠ causal mask. The padding mask hides meaningless positions (<PAD>) and is used wherever attention runs — encoder, decoder, and cross-attention. The causal mask hides the future and exists only in the decoder's self-attention.
Shifting and causal masking are strictly decoder operations. The encoder always processes the complete, unshifted source sequence with unmasked, bidirectional self-attention — the word “cat” may freely attend to “mat”, six positions ahead.
