Teaching takeaway
Plain CNNs→ Multi-scale modules→ Skip connections→ Efficient mobile blocks→ Modern ConvNet blocks

Read the evolution as a sequence of answers to concrete problems:

1. Plain CNNs (LeNet, AlexNet). Establish the template — learnable convolutions extract features, pooling reduces resolution, ReLU + GPUs make deep training practical.

2. Depth & multi-scale (VGG, Inception). VGG shows that stacking small 3×3 filters buys depth cheaply; Inception processes multiple receptive fields in parallel and uses 1×1 convs to control cost.

3. Skip connections (ResNet, DenseNet). Identity/skip paths solve the degradation problem, letting networks reach 100+ layers by making it easy to learn residuals and reuse features.

4. Efficiency for mobile (MobileNet v1/v2, EfficientNet). Factorize convolution into depthwise + pointwise, then wrap it in an inverted residual and balance width/depth/resolution with compound scaling.

5. Modern ConvNets (ConvNeXt). Fold Transformer-era design choices back into a pure ConvNet residual block — proving the block, not just the attention, drove much of the recent progress.
