External Clustering Evaluation Metrics

Use when ground-truth labels exist (even for a small validation subset).

🤝 ARI (Adjusted Rand Index)

Compares whether pairs of samples are grouped together correctly.

  • Range: −1 to 1
  • 1 = Perfect agreement
  • 0 ≈ Random clustering
  • Adjusted to remove agreement that occurs just by chance.

📡 NMI (Normalized Mutual Information)

Measures how much information the clustering shares with the true labels.

  • Range: 0 to 1
  • 1 = Perfect match
  • 0 = No relationship
  • Think: “How much does knowing the cluster tell us about the true class?”
⚖️ Key Takeaway: Unlike internal metrics (like Silhouette Score), ARI and NMI evaluate agreement with the true labels, not the geometric shape or density of the clusters!
