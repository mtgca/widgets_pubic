Types of Sequential Models

Sequence models are classified by the shape of their input and output: many-to-one, one-to-many, and two flavors of many-to-many. Pick a type, choose a concrete example, and step through time to see how inputs (pink) flow through hidden states (green) to produce outputs (blue).

Example:
input xt hidden state ht (memory) output yt

From the definition

    💡 Intuition

    The four types at a glance

    TypeInputOutputAligned in time?Typical tasks
    Many to one
    (x₁…x_T) → y    		 A whole sequence (words, frames, audio) One single element — (one output for the entire sequence) Sentiment analysis, action recognition
    One to many
    x → (y₁…y_T)    		 One element (an image, encoded once into a static embedding) A sequence (words of a caption) — (one input feeds every output step) Image captioning
    Many to many (seq2seq)
    (x₁…x_T) → (y₁…y_T′)    		 A sequence in language 1 A sequence in language 2 — lengths can differ No — encoder reads everything first, then the decoder writes Machine translation
    Many to many (aligned)
    (x₁…x_T) → (y₁…y_T)    		 A sequence (e.g. video frames, words) One output per input element, same length Yes — x_t and y_t correspond one-to-one Frame-level video classification, Named Entity Recognition
