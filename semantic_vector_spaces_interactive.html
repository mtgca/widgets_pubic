Semantic Vector Spaces — The Geometry of Meaning

Each word is mapped to a dense vector: meaning spread across several numeric dimensions. Explore how that single idea gives you clusters (similar words sit together), a ruler (cosine similarity), and even algebra (king→−man→+woman→≈queen→).

4Reality check: what real embeddings look like

⚠ The labels are a pedagogical fiction

Names like "royalty" or "feline" were written by hand for this demo. In practice the dimensions are latent and uninterpretable: they are learned automatically from corpus statistics, and no engineer decides what dimension 3 means. What survives in real embeddings is exactly what you explored here — the geometry: neighborhoods, directions, and analogies.

Scale

Real embeddings use hundreds of dimensions (e.g. 300 for word2vec/GloVe, 768+ inside transformers) over vocabularies of tens of thousands of tokens — far too many numbers to inspect by eye, which is why we reason about them geometrically. How these vectors are learned is a topic of its own — the intuition to keep from here is: meaning ≈ position in a vector space.
