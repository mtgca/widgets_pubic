Expectation-Maximization (EM) for GMMs

The Algorithm

The EM algorithm alternates between guessing the probabilities (E-Step) and updating the cluster shapes (M-Step) until it perfectly fits the data.

1. Initialization

We start by placing Gaussian distributions (ellipses) randomly or poorly over the data.

2. E-Step (Expectation)

"Who belongs to whom?"
Given the current shapes, we calculate the posterior probability for every point. Colors blend based on these soft assignments.

3. M-Step (Maximization)

"Update the shapes."
Using those soft probabilities, we re-calculate the Mean (center), Covariance (size/shape), and Weight for each Gaussian to better fit the points assigned to it.

Notice how it resembles "Soft K-Means". Instead of points voting 100% for one centroid, they cast fractional votes based on probability.
