Dropout

Regularization Technique

  • During training, it randomly "drops" (sets to zero) a fraction of neurons and their connections at each iteration.
  • The dropped values change at each iteration, forcing the model to learn different paths.
  • Prevents "memorization" of information to reduce overfitting.
  • Forces the network to learn more robust and distributed representations.
  • Applied only during training, all nodes are active during inference.
